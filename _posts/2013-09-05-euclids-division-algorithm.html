If \(a\) and \(b\) are integers then \(a,b\) can be expressed as \(a = bq + r\) where \( 0 \leq r < b\)

Euclidean Algorithm to find \(gcd(34,126)\)

\[\begin{aligned} 126 = 3 \cdot 34 + 24 \\ 34 = 1 \cdot 24 + 10 \\ 24 = 2 \cdot 10 + 4 \\ 10 = 2 \cdot 4 + 2 \\ 4 = 2 \cdot 2 + 0\\ gcd(34,126) = 2 \end{aligned}\]

gcd as as linear combination of numbers

If, \(d = gcd(a,b)\), then \(d\) can be written as \(d = ma + kb\). From above,

\[\begin{aligned} 24 = 126 - 3 \cdot 34 \\ 10 = 34 - 1 \cdot 24 \\ 4 = 24 - 2 \cdot 10 \\ 2 = 10 - 2 \cdot 4 \end{aligned}\]

Substituting back,

\[\begin{aligned} 2 = ( 34 -24 ) - 2(24 - 2 \cdot 10) \\ 2 = 34 - 126 + 3 \cdot 34 -2 ( 126 - 3 \cdot 34 - 2( 34 -24)) \\ 2 = 4 \cdot 34 - 126 - 2\cdot126 + 6\cdot34 + 4\cdot34 - 4\cdot24 \\ 2 = 14\cdot34 - 3\cdot126 - 4\cdot 126 + 12\cdot34 \\ 2 = 26\cdot34 - 7\cdot126 \end{aligned}\]
